The Legend of Flik

The story of Flik began as a modest spark of ingenuity within the confines of an AP Research classroom. Seeking a cure to overwhelming boredom, two friends utilized an electric cutter and scraps of cardboard to forge a primitive tabletop game. Dubbed "Flick Chess," the game became an immediate sensation among peers, though it was eventually set aside as the school year transitioned into summer.

The project remained dormant until late February of the following year, when the original prototype was rediscovered. This sparked a period of intense development and craftsmanship. The creators moved beyond cardboard, designing 3D printed pieces, handmaking wood game boards, and crafting custom storage boxes. They further expanded the game with a cast of new characters and refined the mechanics until the game was entirely reshaped and officially christened: Flik.

Mastering the Art of Flickery

The Golden Rules

  • The Strike: Use just one finger to propel your piece.
  • Collision: If you contact an enemy piece during your turn, it is removed from play.
  • The Boundary: Any piece that exits the board is removed from play.
  • Victory: Eliminate all enemy pieces.
